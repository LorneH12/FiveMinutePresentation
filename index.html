No-Bake Cheesecake for Thanksgiving

Slide 1 · Hook

Thanksgiving Dessert in 5 Minutes (of Prep)

Tonight we’ll build a creamy, no-bake cheesecake that chills while you handle the turkey drama.

  • Zero oven time
  • 15 minutes of hands-on work
  • Overnight chill = next-day “wow” moment

Slide 2 · Big Picture

The 3 Phases of the Build

  1. Crust – crunchy, buttery base
  2. Filling – smooth, tangy cream cheese
  3. Chill & Top – the patience & the flex

Every later slide drops into one of these phases, so your learners always know where they are in the recipe.

Slide 3 · Ingredients

The Core Shopping List

Crust

  • Graham cracker crumbs – 2 cups
  • Melted butter – ½ cup
  • Sugar – 2 tbsp

Filling

  • Cream cheese – 24 oz (softened)
  • Heavy cream – 1 cup
  • Powdered sugar – ¾ cup
  • Vanilla & lemon juice

Slide 4 · Step 1

Build the Crust (5 Minutes)

  • Combine crumbs, sugar, and melted butter.
  • Press firmly into a springform pan.
  • Chill the crust while you move on — it firms up as you prep the filling.

Trainer tip: ask the room “Too much crust or not enough?” and get quick opinions.

Slide 5 · Step 2

Whip the Filling

  • Cream the cream cheese until completely smooth.
  • Beat in powdered sugar, vanilla, and lemon juice.
  • Whip cold heavy cream to soft peaks, then fold in.

The goal: light and airy, no lumps. This is the “luxury” moment of the recipe.

Slide 6 · Step 3

Fill, Smooth, and Chill

  • Spread the filling over the chilled crust.
  • Smooth the top with an offset spatula or spoon.
  • Cover and refrigerate at least 6 hours (overnight ideal).

Fridge time is where structure and flavor develop — emphasize this to your learners.

Slide 7 · Toppings

Dress It for Thanksgiving

  • Fresh berries + berry coulis (as on screen)
  • Salted caramel and toasted pecans
  • Spiced cranberry sauce swirl

Pick one hero topping so the visual story is clean and photograph-ready.

Slide 8 · Timing

When to Make What

  • Day before: crust, filling, full chill.
  • Morning of: slice toppings, prep sauces.
  • Just before serving: finish decor + photos.

This keeps your oven clear and your headspace calm on the big day.

Slide 9 · Variations

Flavor Swaps in One Slide

  • Pumpkin spice swirl
  • Chocolate cookie crust
  • Lemon curd layer under the berries

Use this as an interactive moment — have participants pitch their own twist.

Slide 10 · Pitfalls

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not softening cream cheese fully
  • Whipping the cream too little (or too much)
  • Rushing the chill time

Each mistake is a teaching story — this is where you fold in real-world examples.

Slide 11 · Recap

From Empty Counter to Finished Dessert

  1. Crust mixed and pressed
  2. Filling whipped and folded
  3. Cake assembled and chilled
  4. Toppings added with intention

By now the learner can tell the story of the build in four simple beats.
