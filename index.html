Slide 3 · Ingredients

The Core Shopping List

Crust

  • Graham cracker crumbs – 2 cups
  • Melted butter – ½ cup
  • Sugar – 2 tbsp

Filling

  • Cream cheese – 24 oz (softened)
  • Heavy cream – 1 cup
  • Powdered sugar – ¾ cup
  • Vanilla & lemon juice

Presenter tip: pause here; check that everyone understands the difference between “prep ahead” items and “same-day” items.

Slide 4 · Gear

Tools You Actually Need

  • 9-inch springform pan (or pie dish in a pinch)
  • Mixing bowls + rubber spatula
  • Hand mixer or stand mixer
  • Plastic wrap + fridge space

Keep the list light – the “I already own this” feeling reduces friction and makes the recipe feel more actionable.

Slide 5 · Crust

Step 1 · Build the Crust

  1. Combine graham crumbs, sugar, and melted butter.
  2. Press firmly into the bottom of the pan.
  3. Chill for 10–15 minutes to set.

Narrate texture cues: “It should feel like wet sand, not soup.”

Slide 6 · Filling

Step 2 · Whip the Filling

  1. Beat cream cheese until smooth and fluffy.
  2. Add powdered sugar, vanilla, and lemon.
  3. Whip heavy cream separately to soft peaks.
  4. Fold whipped cream into cream cheese mixture.

Slide 7 · Assembly

Step 3 · Pour, Smooth, Chill

  • Pour filling over the chilled crust.
  • Smooth the top with a spatula.
  • Cover and chill for at least 4 hours – ideally overnight.

This is where you emphasize: “We’re trading oven time for fridge time.”

Slide 8 · Toppings

Finishing Moves

  • Fresh berries & mint
  • Berry sauce drizzle
  • Chocolate shavings or caramel swirl

Keep the dessert mostly neutral; let guests customize slices at the table.

Slide 9 · Timing

Thanksgiving Timeline

  • 2 days before: Shop & clear fridge space.
  • 1 day before: Make crust + filling; chill overnight.
  • Day of: Add toppings 30–60 minutes before serving.

Slide 10 · Risks

Common Pitfalls & Fixes

  • Lumpy filling: cream cheese wasn’t softened.
  • Soupy texture: whipped cream was under-whipped.
  • Soggy crust: butter ratio too high, or not chilled.
